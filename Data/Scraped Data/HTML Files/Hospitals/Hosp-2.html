    National AdvantageTM Program (NAP)

    NAP stands for National Advantage Program. Providers that are in NAP are not network providers and they are not paid at the Preferred Benefit level. You may get a discount if you use a NAP doctor. However, a discount cannot be guaranteed under the NAP for any particular claim.
    That means you are likely to pay more for a NAP doctor than an Aetna in-network doctor. To pay less, use a doctor in the Aetna network. Please note that Aetna relies on third parties to report changes in provider participation in the NAP. So NAP provider information is updated much less frequently than is the case with Aetna networks. Always check with a NAP provider to confirm that they are current NAP program participants.
